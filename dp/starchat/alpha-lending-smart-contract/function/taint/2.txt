User controllability is defined as the capability of an entity to control the use and management of information, data, and systems. In the context of IT, user controlability refers to the ability of individuals to determine how information is processed and used.
In general, information technology (IT) is any branch of engineering that deals with the collection, processing, maintenance, use, sharing, dissemination, or disposal of data or information. IT is also known as information and communications technology, which is often abbreviated as ICT. Information and data can be anything from numbers and letters to images and sounds. For example, a person's name, social security number, credit card number and address are all pieces of personally identifiable information (PII).
