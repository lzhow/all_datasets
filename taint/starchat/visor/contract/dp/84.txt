I'm not sure what you mean by "good".  There are many good ways to start learning how  to program in Python.  The best way will depend on your current level of experience and the type of program you want to write.

If you are new to programming, you can start by learning the basics of Python, such as variables, data types, loops, and functions. These foundational skills will help you write simple programs and understand how the language works. 

Once you have the basic skills, it's a great idea to move on to more advanced topics, like classes, objects, modules, packages, inheritance, etc. This will give you a strong foundation for building more complex programs.
