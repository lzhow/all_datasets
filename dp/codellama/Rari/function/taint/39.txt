This is an external call, and the result of the call is determined by an untrusted contract. Therefore, the outcome of this call cannot be controlled directly by a user, but rather by another contract that may have malicious intentions.

In general, when a contract makes an internal call to another address, there is no guarantee that it will succeed or that any data returned from that call will be accurate. This means that a maliciously designed contract could potentially exploit this vulnerability to execute arbitrary code or manipulate the state of other contracts.
